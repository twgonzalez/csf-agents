The Broken Narrative

Sacramento Sold You a Lie.

Build more luxury towers and prices will fall. Strip cities of planning authority and working families will benefit. Eight years of data say otherwise — and the lobbyists funding Sacramento's housing bills know it.

This is not an anti-housing campaign. It is a pro-community, pro-affordability campaign. We want working families to stay in their neighborhoods. We want cities to decide what that looks like — not Wall Street.

Three Myths Exposed
✕ "Build More and Prices Will Fall"
Market-rate towers in high-demand urban cores do not filter down to working families. They accelerate land speculation and displace the people they claim to help.
✕ "Local Zoning Causes the Crisis"
California's affordability crisis predates the local control era. It is driven by investor ownership, interest rates, and chronic underinvestment in truly affordable housing — not city planning boards.
✕ "Sacramento Knows Best"
One-size-fits-all state mandates ignore vast differences between a coastal city, a suburb, and a rural community. Local governance exists because communities are not interchangeable.
The Solution

Why a Ballot Initiative Is the Only Path Forward

Lawsuits fail. Electing new legislators hasn't worked. Sacramento closes every door with a new law. A citizen constitutional initiative is the one mechanism they cannot block.

  1. 1

    Signature Collection

    We gather verified signatures from California voters to place the initiative on the statewide ballot.

  2. 2

    Statewide Ballot Vote

    California voters decide directly. Not lobbyists, not developer PACs. A simple majority passes the measure.

  3. 3

    Local Authority Restored

    The 400+ overriding state laws become opt-in. Cities choose what fits — and opt out of what doesn't.

  4. 4

    Affordable Housing Prioritized

    State housing investment redirects to income-qualified, deed-restricted units — not luxury towers that satisfy state metrics while pricing out families.
