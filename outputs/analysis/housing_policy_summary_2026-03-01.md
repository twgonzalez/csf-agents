### Key Bills to Watch

**[AB1751](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB1751)** — Missing Middle Townhome Ownership Act.  
*Sharon Quirk-Silva | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C** **D***  
Creates a statewide ministerial pathway for townhomes on any residential parcel, directly preempting local zoning and eliminating all discretionary review; cities cannot require impact fees or infrastructure improvements for new townhome residents.

**[SB1014](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260SB1014)** — Development projects: onsite and offsite improvements.  
*Tim Grayson | Introduced | Criteria: **A** B C **D***  
The bill's title — "Development projects: onsite and offsite improvements" — and its addition of new Government Code sections (65913.3.2 and 65941.3) strongly suggest it limits the conditions cities may impose on developers regarding on- and off-site infrastructure improvements (e.g., road dedications, utility extensions, frontage work). This directly restricts local impact fee and improvement-condition authority, a hallmark local control risk. The pairing of the new sections with existing housing streamlining statutes (Ch. 65913 series) implies ministerial or by-right approval elements that reduce discretionary review and partially preempt local zoning standards for qualifying projects.

**[AB1997](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB1997)** — Land use: housing development approvals: timelines and processes.  
*Alex Lee | Introduced | Criteria: A B C D*  
AB 1997 amends the Housing Accountability Act (65589.5), anti-NIMBY enforcement (65589.9), and permit streamlining timelines (65950), while adding new mandatory approval timeline provisions (65943.3) and a Health and Safety Code enforcement mechanism (50411). These sections collectively compress local review windows, restrict the conditions and findings cities may use to deny or modify housing applications, and impose state-level process mandates that override local deliberative authority — presenting meaningful local control risk across all four criteria even prior to committee amendments.

**[AB2005](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2005)** — Housing developments: urban lot split: parcel maps.  
*Patrick Ahrens | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C** D*  
AB 2005 amends Government Code §66411.7, the urban lot split provision enacted under SB 9, which already mandates ministerial (no-discretion) approval of parcel maps for lot splits in single-family zones statewide. Any amendment to this section is highly likely to expand, clarify, or strengthen that ministerial approval framework — further preempting local zoning authority, eliminating discretionary review, and compelling cities to approve lot splits and associated two-unit developments beyond what local planning would otherwise allow. The ministerial approval structure inherently removes CEQA review and public hearing rights for qualifying parcels, and increased lot splits create incremental infrastructure and permitting capacity pressure on city systems without guaranteed fee offsets.

**[AB2118](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2118)** — Affordable Housing and High Road Jobs Act of 2022: use by right: objective standards.  
*Josh Hoover | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C** D*  
AB 2118 amends the Affordable Housing and High Road Jobs Act of 2022 (Gov. Code §§65912.101, 65912.123), which already establishes a use-by-right, ministerial approval pathway for qualifying affordable housing projects — explicitly overriding local zoning and eliminating discretionary review. The bill's amendment to "objective standards" language likely further constrains the conditions cities may apply to qualifying projects, tightening state preemption of local design, land use, and development review authority. Cities bear compliance and permitting costs with no identified state offset for ministerial processing burdens.

**[AB2288](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2288)** — Adaptive reuse: industrial zones.  
*Sharon Quirk-Silva | Introduced | Criteria: **A** B **C** D*  
Forces cities to allow residential conversions in industrial zones, preempting employment land protections; industrial zones lack residential utility infrastructure and cities cannot require developers to fund water, sewer, pedestrian, or parks upgrades needed for residential occupancy.

**[AB2342](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2342)** — Housing development: transit-oriented development.  
*Josh Hoover | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C** D*  
AB 2342 amends Government Code §65912.157, which sits within California's Transit-Oriented Development (TOD) law. Existing TOD law grants ministerial, by-right approval for qualifying housing projects near transit — explicitly preempting local zoning, eliminating discretionary review and CEQA, and mandating approval of density beyond what local plans may allow. An amendment to this section most likely expands the scope, lowers thresholds, or tightens compliance obligations, all of which carry strong local control risk. Full text is needed to confirm the specific mechanism, but the statutory context alone warrants strong scores on preemption, review removal, and development mandates, with a moderate infrastructure burden from ministerial approvals that prevent cities from conditioning projects on utility or frontage improvements.

**[AB2390](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2390)** — Streamlined housing approvals: hazardous waste sites.  
*Pilar Schiavo | Introduced | Criteria: A B C D*  
AB 2390 amends Government Code §65913.4 — the SB 35 streamlined ministerial approval statute — to extend its by-right approval process to projects on hazardous waste sites. This expands state preemption of local discretionary review to a site category that cities currently retain authority to condition or deny based on contamination concerns, public safety, and remediation adequacy. Cities could lose the ability to impose site-specific conditions tied to cleanup verification, and ministerial approval could force approvals before remediation is complete or certified adequate.

**[AB2433](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2433)** — Housing development: affordable homes bonus.  
*David Alvarez | Introduced | Criteria: A B C D*  
AB 2433 amends Government Code Section 65915 — the core Density Bonus Law statute — and is titled an "affordable homes bonus," strongly suggesting it expands density bonus entitlements, streamlines approval, or broadens eligibility criteria. Amendments to §65915 typically preempt local zoning by requiring cities to grant additional density and reduced parking/development standards beyond what local plans allow, and often include ministerial or limited-discretion approval pathways for qualifying projects. The summary is too brief to confirm the exact mechanism; full bill text is needed to determine whether the expansion is substantial (strong) or incremental (moderate).

**[AB2601](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2601)** — Planning and zoning: housing development: streamlined approval and subdivisions.  
*Alex Lee | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C** D*  
AB 2601 amends multiple streamlined approval and subdivision statutes (§§65852.21, 65852.28, 65913.4.5, 66411.7, 66499.41) that collectively mandate ministerial/by-right approvals, limit local discretionary review, and expand subdivision entitlements — all of which directly preempt city and county zoning and planning authority. The combination of streamlining and subdivision amendments is likely to restrict the conditions and standards cities may impose on qualifying projects, creating an indirect infrastructure and capacity burden where local mitigation authority may be curtailed.
