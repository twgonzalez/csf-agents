### Key Bills to Watch

**[AB1751](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB1751)** — Missing Middle Townhome Ownership Act.  
*Sharon Quirk-Silva | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C***  
Missing Middle Townhome Ownership Act — establishes a new by-right/ministerial pathway for townhome production (Section 65852.29); directly enables densification on existing parcels and removes local discretion to deny qualifying townhome projects.

**[AB2005](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2005)** — Housing developments: urban lot split: parcel maps.  
*Patrick Ahrens | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C***  
Amends urban lot split provisions (Section 66411.7, SB 9 implementation); likely expands or clarifies ministerial lot splitting to enable more housing units per parcel, directly densifying existing neighborhoods with reduced local discretion.

**[AB2079](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2079)** — Adaptive reuse.  
*Sade Elhawary | Introduced | Criteria: **A** B C*  
Establishes or expands adaptive reuse provisions for converting nonresidential buildings to housing; likely includes streamlined/ministerial approval pathway, directly creating new residential units and enabling higher-density residential use.

**[AB2127](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2127)** — Accessory dwelling units.  
*Natasha Johnson | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C***  
Expands or modifies ADU provisions; ADU approvals are already ministerial under state law, and this bill likely further reduces barriers or expands eligibility, directly enabling densification on existing single-family parcels.

**[AB2252](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2252)** — Housing: Single stair entry and exit.  
*Alex Lee | Introduced | Criteria: A **B** C*  
Removes building code restriction requiring dual stairways in certain multifamily buildings; directly enables more efficient high-density floor plans, allowing taller/narrower buildings on smaller urban lots.

**[AB2288](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2288)** — Adaptive reuse: industrial zones.  
*Sharon Quirk-Silva | Introduced | Criteria: **A** B **C***  
Expands adaptive reuse provisions to industrial zones (Sections 65658.1, 65658.5); likely establishes ministerial approval for converting industrial buildings to housing, directly increasing production and removing local discretion over qualifying conversions.

**[AB2295](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2295)** — Regional housing need: affordable housing.  
*Natasha Johnson | Introduced | Criteria: A C D*  
Adds new affordable housing provisions to RHNA process (Section 65584.2.3); affects how local governments plan for affordable units, with state mandate compliance costs and potential penalties.

**[AB2320](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2320)** — Multifamily Housing Program: Homekey: adaptive reuse.  
*Tri Ta | Introduced | Criteria: **A** B C*  
Combines Multifamily Housing Program funding with adaptive reuse for Homekey projects; directly accelerates conversion of nonresidential buildings to housing through a streamlined state-funded pathway.

**[AB2342](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2342)** — Housing development: transit-oriented development.  
*Josh Hoover | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** **C***  
Amends transit-oriented development by-right provisions (Section 65912.157); likely expands qualifying areas or removes restrictions on TOD housing, enabling high-density residential near transit with reduced local discretion.

**[AB2433](https://leginfo.legislature.ca.gov/faces/billStatusClient.xhtml?bill_id=202520260AB2433)** — Housing development: affordable homes bonus.  
*David Alvarez | Introduced | Criteria: **A** **B** C*  
Expands density bonus law affordable homes bonus (Section 65915); directly allows denser affordable housing development with state override of local density limits, a core production incentive.
